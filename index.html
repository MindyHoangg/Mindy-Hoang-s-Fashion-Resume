Mindy Hoang

Fashion Designer

As an undergraduate student, I’ve had several experiences in the fashion industry in order to become a fashion designer with a specialty in bridal gowns. My fashion journey began in high school, when I took a fashion course to learn how to sew. Since then, I’ve sewn many different fashionable pieces such as pants, shirts, dresses, a tulle skirt, and even pillows! From here, I decided to expand my knowledge and vowed that once I go to college, I needed to gain more experiences in the fashion industry.

When I started to attend the University of California, Riverside, I applied to various campus ambassador programs. From my applications, I was first accepted as a SHIEN ambassador! SHEIN is a fashion company that’s growing quickly and popular amongst the younger generation. As a shien ambassador, my job was to post Tik Toks of different fashionable pieces, take product shots of clothes and accessories, and bring SHIEN on campus by posting photos, flyers, and sales. I was then also accepted as a VS PINK Campus Representative for the University of California, Riverside! This is my dream job, and I couldn’t be more elated having to experience this. As VS PINK Campus Representative, I was in charge of planning and executing social media marketing strategies and events on campus for The University of California, Riverside PINK's social media platforms, collaborate with a Co-Representative to plan and host bi-weekly events both in person and virtually to increase student engagement and build brand awareness on University of California, Riverside, manage and guide an additional team of 5 Campus Team Members to help coordinate and plan events, as well as oversee Campus Team Members' content creation for social media channels! These new skill sets definitely allowed me to expand on my experiences which is a crucial asset in the fashion industry.

Outside of college, I’ve also had the opportunity to pursue fashion. For example, I’ve been •a wardrobe manager for Thien An Performing Arts. I am essentially in charge of promoting and performing in significant cultural events; such as the Tet Parade, Fundraisers, and Vietnamese Military Appreciation Events, coordinating the traditional dresses and outfits worn in each performance while helping other members with the routine and setting up positions when needed. Being involved with this group for about 5 years now, which is truly rewarding. Additionally, I was a stylist for a boutique called Dixie’s Boutique, where I was in charge of picking out outfits for models during the Photoshoot, helped the photographed with poses, fixed hair and makeup, and was the right hand for the CEO. With these experiences, I am confident that I would be a great addition to any team out there.
